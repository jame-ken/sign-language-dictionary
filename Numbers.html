ASL Numbers & Tens

Understanding the handshapes for cardinal numbers and larger values.

1-5

Counting 1 to 5

For 1 through 5, your palm faces inward towards your face. Note that '3' uses the thumb, index, and middle finger.

Usage: Ordering "4 coffees" or saying "I have 2 brothers."

6-9

Counting 6 to 9

For 6 through 9, your palm faces outward. These are formed by touching a finger to your thumb (6=pinky, 7=ring, 8=middle, 9=index).

Usage: Giving a phone number or saying "The bus arrives in 7 minutes."

10

The Thumb Shake

Extend your thumb up (like a 'thumbs up') and shake your hand side-to-side slightly.

Usage: Stating a quantity of ten or the end of a basic count.

20

The Pinch

Touch your thumb and index finger together repeatedly (like a bird beak pinching).

Usage: Saying "I am 20 years old" or "It costs 20 dollars."

30-60

The "Zero" Ending

Form the base number (3, 4, 5, or 6) and then immediately transition the hand into an 'O' shape for zero.

Usage: Used for ages, prices, and addresses. 30 is '3' then '0'.

100

[ IMAGE OF 100 ]

1 + C

Sign the number '1' and then pull your hand back while changing it into the 'C' handshape (representing the Roman numeral for 100).

Usage: Measuring "100 miles" or counting large sums.

ASL Number Rules Summary

  • Palm Orientation (1-5): When counting objects, the palm faces inward. If signing a date or time, it may face outward.
  • Palm Orientation (6-9): The palm always faces outward toward the listener.
  • The "3" Rule: In ASL, the number 3 uses the thumb, index, and middle fingers. Using the index, middle, and ring fingers (the English way) is a common beginner error.
  • The Tens (30-90): These are signed as two distinct movements: the first digit followed by the 'O' (zero) shape.
  • Movement (20): Unlike other tens, 20 has a unique "pinching" movement rather than a 2-0 sequence.
  • Hundreds (100+): Uses the number + the letter 'C' (e.g., 200 is '2' then 'C').
