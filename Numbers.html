ASL Numbers Guide

Visual reference for counting and large values.

1 through 5

Palm faces inward. Remember that '3' is signed with the thumb, index, and middle finger.

6 through 9

Palm faces outward. Fingers touch the thumb to distinguish each value.

Ten

Extend the thumb upward and shake the hand side-to-side.

Twenty

Repeatedly pinch the thumb and index finger together.

Thirty to Sixty

Sign the base number followed immediately by the 'O' shape for zero.

One Hundred

Sign '1' and pull the hand back while forming a 'C' shape.

General ASL Number Rules

  • Numbers 1-5: Keep your palm facing inward toward yourself.
  • Numbers 6-9: Flip your hand so your palm faces outward toward the listener.
  • The Number 10: Think of a "thumbs up" and give it a little shake; this marks the end of your basic 1-10 count.
  • The "3" Rule: In ASL, the number 3 always uses the thumb. Avoid using your ring finger.
  • Tens & Hundreds: For 30-90, sign the first number then a '0'. For 100, sign '1' then move into a 'C' shape.
