The ASL Alphabet

The foundation of fingerspelling. Keep your palm facing out!

A

How to Sign 'A'

Make a fist and keep your thumb straight up against the side of your index finger.

Pro Tip: Ensure your thumb is on the side, not tucked in front, to avoid confusing it with 'S'.

B

How to Sign 'B'

Open your hand with all fingers touching and tuck your thumb across your palm.

Fun Fact: This handshape is also used for the sign for "Blue"!

C

How to Sign 'C'

Curve your fingers and thumb to form the shape of the letter 'C'.

Usage: As you learned in the numbers section, this shape also represents "100".

D

How to Sign 'D'

Point your index finger up and touch the tips of your other fingers to your thumb to form a circle.

Careful: Don't confuse this with 'F', where the index and thumb touch.

E

How to Sign 'E'

Curl all your fingers in so the tips touch your thumb, which is tucked underneath.

Tip: Think of it like a "scrunchy" handshape.
