This instructional image illustrates the letters of the ASL manual alphabet used for finger spelling. Each letter requires a specific, static handshape and palm orientation to be clearly understood; for example, 'A' is a fist with the thumb beside the index finger, 'L' forms the letter with the index finger and thumb, and 'N' is a fist with the thumb tucked under the middle and ring fingers. While most letters are static, the letter 'J' is uniquely shown as a movement, beginning as the 'I' handshape before curving down and outward.
