American Sign Language Alphabet

ASL is a visual language. Fingerspelling is used for names and words without a specific sign.

A

Image A

Fist with thumb on the side.

B

Image B

Flat palm, thumb tucked in.

C

Image C

Curved hand forming a 'C'.

D

Image D

Index up, others form a circle.

E

Image E

Fingers curled onto thumb.

F

Image F

Thumb/index touch, others up.

G

Image G

Thumb/index point sideways.

H

Image H

Index/middle point sideways.

I

Image I

Pinky finger extended up.

J

Image J

Pinky draws a 'J' curve.

K

Image K

Index/middle up, thumb between.

L

Image L

Index/thumb form an 'L'.

M

Image M

Thumb under three fingers.

N

Image N

Thumb under two fingers.

O

Image O

All fingers touch thumb.

P

Image P

Downwards 'K' handshape.

Q

Image Q

Downwards 'G' handshape.

R

Image R

Index and middle crossed.

S

Image S

Fist with thumb over fingers.

T

Image T

Thumb under index finger.

U

Image U

Index/middle extended together.

V

Image V

Index/middle spread in a 'V'.

W

Image W

Three fingers spread up.

X

Image X

Index finger hooked.

Y

Image Y

Thumb and pinky extended.

Z

Image Z
