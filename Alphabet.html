The ASL Alphabet

The foundation of fingerspelling. Keep your palm facing out!

A

The 'A' Handshape

Close your hand into a fist with the thumb resting along the side of the index finger.

Tip: Keep the thumb on the outside. Tucking it inside makes it look like an 'S'.

B

The 'B' Handshape

Keep your four fingers straight up and touching, with your thumb tucked across the palm.

Tip: Keep your palm flat and facing directly forward.

C

The 'C' Handshape

Curve your fingers and thumb to resemble the letter 'C'.

Tip: Imagine you are holding a soda can.

D

The 'D' Handshape

Point your index finger up while your middle, ring, and pinky fingers touch your thumb.

Common Mistake: Don't touch your index finger to your thumb, or it becomes an 'F'.

E

The 'E' Handshape

Curl all fingers so the tips rest on top of your thumb, which is tucked across the palm.

Tip: Keep the "claws" tight to distinguish it from a lazy 'O'.

F

The 'F' Handshape

Touch your index finger and thumb together to form a circle, and fan out your other three fingers.

Tip: It looks like the "Okay" gesture.

G

The 'G' Handshape

Point your index finger and thumb to the side, parallel to each other.

Tip: Imagine you are indicating a small distance between your fingers.

H

The 'H' Handshape

Extend both your index and middle fingers horizontally to the side.

Tip: Think of 'G' but with an extra finger.

I

The 'I' Handshape

Close your hand into a fist and extend only your pinky finger straight up.

Tip: Keep your thumb tucked in front of your fingers.

J

The 'J' Handshape

Make the 'I' handshape and use your pinky to draw the shape of a 'J' in the air.

Tip: The motion starts with the palm facing you and ends with it facing slightly more outward.

K

The 'K' Handshape

Point your index finger up, your middle finger forward at an angle, and place your thumb on the middle finger knuckle.

Tip: It looks like a "Peace" sign with a thumb in the middle.

L

The 'L' Handshape

Extend your thumb and index finger to form an 'L' shape.

Tip: This is one of the easiest signs to remember!

M

The 'M' Handshape

Tuck your thumb under your index, middle, and ring fingers.

Tip: Think of the three fingers representing the three humps in a lowercase 'm'.

N

The 'N' Handshape

Tuck your thumb under your index and middle fingers.

Tip: Only two fingers over the thumb for 'N'.

O

The 'O' Handshape

Touch all your fingertips to your thumb to form an 'O' shape.

Tip: Make sure it's a clear circle.

P

The 'P' Handshape

This is a 'K' handshape but pointed downwards.

Tip: Index points to the floor, middle finger points forward.

Q

The 'Q' Handshape

This is a 'G' handshape but pointed downwards.

Tip: Imagine you are about to pick up a small piece of paper from the ground.

R

The 'R' Handshape

Cross your index and middle fingers.

Tip: Like "fingers crossed" for good luck.

S

The 'S' Handshape

Make a fist and place your thumb directly in front of your fingers.

Difference: In 'A', the thumb is on the side; in 'S', it is on the front.

T

The 'T' Handshape

Make a fist and tuck your thumb under your index finger.

Tip: Only one finger (index) goes over the thumb.

U

The 'U' Handshape

Extend your index and middle fingers straight up and keep them touching.

Tip: Keep those two fingers glued together!

V

The 'V' Handshape

Extend your index and middle fingers straight up in a 'V' shape.

Tip: Like a peace sign.

W

The 'W' Handshape

Extend your index, middle, and ring fingers in a 'W' shape.

Tip: The thumb touches the pinky.

X

The 'X' Handshape

Make a fist and then curl your index finger into a hook shape.

Tip: Think of Captain Hook's hand.

Y

The 'Y' Handshape

Extend your thumb and pinky finger while keeping the middle fingers tucked.

Tip: Looks like a "Hang Loose" sign.

Z

The 'Z' Handshape

Use your index finger to draw the letter 'Z' in the air.

Tip: Trace the shape exactly as you would write it.
