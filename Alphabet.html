This chart shows the standard ASL signs for numbers 1–20. In ASL, numbers use specific handshapes, palm orientations, and small movements. Numbers 1–5 usually face toward the signer, while 6–9 use the thumb touching different fingers. The number 10 is made with a small thumb shake. Numbers 11–15 include a repeated downward flicking movement, which helps distinguish them from other signs. These forms are commonly used across the Deaf community and provide the foundation for learning larger numbers and number-based signs in ASL.
