The Roots of ASL

American Sign Language is a complete, natural language with its own unique grammar and syntax, distinct from English. Its history is a powerful story of community and resilience.

Origins & Influence

In 1817, Thomas Hopkins Gallaudet and Laurent Clerc (a deaf educator from France) founded the first school for the deaf in Hartford, CT. ASL evolved from a beautiful blend of French Sign Language (LSF) and various indigenous "village signs" used across early America.

Cultural Identity

ASL is the heart of Deaf Culture. Despite historical attempts to suppress signing in favor of speech, the Deaf community has fought to preserve ASL as a sophisticated linguistic system. It is a visual expression of identity, history, and shared experience.

Pro-Tips for New Signers

Eye Contact

In ASL, eye contact is grammar. Looking away during a conversation is considered dismissive, much like walking away mid-sentence.

Use Your Face

Facial expressions act as the "adverbs" and "adjectives" of ASL. Your face tells the listener if you are asking a question or making a statement.

Dominant Hand

Consistency is key. Use your writing hand for primary movements. Switching hands mid-conversation is like changing your accent every five seconds!
