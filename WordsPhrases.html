Words & Phrases

Master high-frequency signs for daily social interactions.

Hello

The Sign for Hello

Touch the tips of your fingers to your forehead near your temple, then move your hand outward and away in a motion similar to a salute.

Usage Tip: This is a formal greeting. For friends, a simple wave (Goodbye shape) is often used as a casual "Hi."

Please

The Sign for Please

Place your flat hand (palm facing in) on the center of your chest and move it in a circular motion clockwise.

Usage Tip: This sign is essential for polite interaction. Using a larger circle can emphasize how much you really want something!

Thanks

The Sign for Thank You

Touch your fingertips to your chin with a flat hand, then move your hand forward and down toward the person you are talking to.

Common Error: Be careful not to start from the lips, as that can be confused with the sign for "Kiss." Start strictly at the chin.

Yes

The Sign for Yes

Make an "S" handshape (a fist) and tilt it up and down at the wrist, mimicking the motion of a head nodding.

Usage Tip: Always nod your head slightly while signing this to reinforce the affirmative meaning.

No

The Sign for No

Snap your index and middle fingers down onto your thumb. It looks like a bird's beak snapping shut.

Usage Tip: Give your head a small shake "no" simultaneously to ensure your message is clear.
